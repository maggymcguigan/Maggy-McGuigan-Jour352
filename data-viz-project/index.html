Female athletes' struggling throughout the pandemic

A deep dive into Division I athletes' mental health challenges throughout the pandemic, provided by the NCAA.

By Maggy McGuigan

Division I female athletes' had immense mental health struggles throughout the pandemic, according to a study by the NCAA. Studies show feelings of being overwhelmed, sad, exhausted, and stressed about keeping up with school while playing a sport. In the height of the pandemic, many female student athletes worried about completing school work as the weight of mental health issues impacted their well being. Additionally, when compared to Division I men's athletes, women had more worries about factors like academics, planning for the future, and thier team environment, the NCAA found.
